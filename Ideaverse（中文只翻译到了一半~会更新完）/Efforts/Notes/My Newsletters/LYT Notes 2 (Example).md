## On Obsidian “Timelapse” 关于黑曜石“延时摄影”

Some features change the game, others just make it more fun. Obsidian “Timelapse” is definitely in the “fun” category. Here’s my [3-minute showcase on Youtube](https://youtu.be/9NyOGXOTmUc) of four different vaults set to some Norwegian’s music (maybe you’ve heard it before?).
有些功能改变了游戏，有些只是让它更有趣。黑曜石“延时摄影”绝对属于“有趣”类别。这是我在 Youtube 上展示的 3 分钟展示的四个不同的金库，设置了一些挪威音乐（也许你以前听过？

[![](https://embed.filekitcdn.com/e/dv87Nny89souiCFyZqnEgh/rK4Gg4drcUasKhsHNaH5mb/email)](https://youtu.be/9NyOGXOTmUc)

How to do a timelapse? Go to the graph view, hit Command-P (on a Mac), and start typing “timelapse” until you see “Start graph timelapse animation”, then hit enter. The animation will start. 如何进行延时摄影？转到图形视图，按 Command-P（在 Mac 上），然后开始键入“延时摄影”，直到看到“开始图形延时摄影动画”，然后按回车键。动画将开始。

## On why everyone believed the book that said the moon doubled in size each year关于为什么每个人都相信那本书说月亮的大小每年翻一番

Ptolemy was a Greek guy living around 150 CE in Alexandria, Egypt, where the biggest library in the world was.
托勒密是一个希腊人，大约在公元150年生活在埃及亚历山大，那里是世界上最大的图书馆。

And so Ptolemy did what any self-respecting librarian and star-lover would do: He read and stared at the stars…a lot.
因此，托勒密做了任何有自尊心的图书管理员和星光爱好者都会做的事情：他阅读并凝视星空......好多。

All that reading and staring led to something massive. He assembled and created a unified theory of the cosmos known as “The Almagest” (which basically means “The Greatest”). This text was so good, it was treated as 100% true for 1500 years!
所有这些阅读和凝视导致了一些巨大的事情。他组装并创造了一个统一的宇宙理论，称为“天文学大成”（基本上意味着“最伟大的”）。这段文字太好了，1500年来一直被视为100%真实！

Except, for it to have been 100% true, the Moon was supposed to double in size and then shrink back each year! But, it doesn’t. So why did really smart people believe in The Almagest? Because it could consistently tell you where the stars would be at any time of the year. (So who cares about that Moon business!)
除了，对于它是100%真实的，月球的大小应该翻一番，然后每年缩小！但是，事实并非如此。那么，为什么真正聪明的人会相信《天文学大成》呢？因为它可以始终如一地告诉你一年中任何时候星星的位置。（所以谁在乎那个月亮生意！

Even after Copernicus published his heliocentric theory in 1543 (casting major doubt on The Almagest), it took another century until Kepler did what no one else could: he gave up his deeply ingrained beliefs. Kepler didn’t just think outside the box, he thought outside the circle! (That story is for another time.)
即使在哥白尼于1543年发表了他的日心说（对《天文学大成》产生了重大怀疑）之后，开普勒又过了一个世纪才做到了其他人无法做到的事情：他放弃了他根深蒂固的信念。开普勒不只是跳出框框思考，他跳出圈子思考！（这个故事是另一个时间。

The Almagest is the centerpiece of a fascinating exploration—not into the stars—but into how we form our beliefs. Here are some personal thoughts on the strangeness of what we believe:
《天文学大成》是一场引人入胜的探索的核心——不是对星星的探索——而是对我们如何形成我们的信仰。以下是关于我们所相信的奇怪之处的一些个人想法：

-   The Almagest shows our ability to force fit incorrect solutions, and then believe those solutions (ignoring the inconvenient parts).天文学大成展示了我们强制采用不正确的解决方案的能力，然后相信这些解决方案（忽略不方便的部分）。
-   The Almagest shows how we conflate _some_ truth with the _whole_ truth.《天文学大成》展示了我们如何将一些真理与全部真理混为一谈。
-   When reality disproves our beliefs, it’s easier to change the narrative protecting our beliefs, than to actually change our beliefs.当现实反驳我们的信仰时，改变保护我们信仰的叙事比实际改变我们的信仰更容易。
-   We just believe whatever we want to believe.我们只是相信我们想相信的任何东西。

It makes me wonder: 这让我想知道：

-   What blind spots are we each living in right now?
- 我们现在每个人都生活在哪些盲点？
