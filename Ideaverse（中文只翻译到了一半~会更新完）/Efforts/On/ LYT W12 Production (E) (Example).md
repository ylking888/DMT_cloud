> # PKM has officially arrived at UCLA
> PKM正式抵达加州大学洛杉矶分校
> 
> Yesterday was the official start to ushering in PKM and linked thinking at UCLA!昨天是加州大学洛杉矶分校正式开始引入PKM和链接思维！
> 
> We have a group working on everything from plays, short films, essays, sitcoms, books, articles, and pitch decks.我们有一个小组，从事戏剧、短片、散文、情景喜剧、书籍、文章和宣传平台的所有工作。
> 
> |   |
> |---|
> |![](https://embed.filekitcdn.com/e/dv87Nny89souiCFyZqnEgh/ccFkFhY9DFmuWZaR3djrRK/email)<br><br>Using my **ideaverse** at the annual UCLA Bootcamp|在一年一度的加州大学洛杉矶分校训练营
> 
> The UCLA class is a much more intimate group than the Linking Your Thinking Workshop. But I deeply enjoy both for different reasons.
> 加州大学洛杉矶分校的课程是一个比链接你的思维研讨会更亲密的小组。但出于不同的原因，我非常喜欢两者。
> 
> Personally, I'm thrilled that the frameworks I've taught and refined over 14 LYT Workshops made an impact for writers who are _**brand new**_ to PKM.就我个人而言，我很高兴我在 14 个 LYT 研讨会上教授和改进的框架对刚接触 PKM 的作家产生了影响。
> 
> It's become this simple...变得这么简单...
> 
> I just introduce three frameworks to empower us: 我只介绍三个框架来增强我们的能力：
> 
> - **Gardeners and Architects**: how we approach problems园丁和建筑师：我们如何处理问题
> - **Idea Emergence** (notes, maps, home): how our ideas grow想法出现（笔记，地图，主页）：我们的想法如何成长
> - **ARC** (add, relate, communicate): how we get stuckARC（添加、关联、沟通）：我们如何陷入困境
> 
> This leads to a cascade of self-understanding. "Oh, that's how I work; I'm a **gardener**...but my partner is an **architect**...I can actually understand her so much better now!"这导致了一连串的自我理解。“哦，这就是我的工作方式;我是一名园丁...但我的搭档是一名建筑师...我现在真的可以更了解她了！
> 
> I explained **Idea Emergence**, I concluded by saying, "But in the end, there are just three levels (notes, maps, home). When you get stuck gardening, go back to the map. When you get stuck architecting, you have to get back into the weeds."我解释了Idea Emergence，我最后说：“但最终，只有三个层次（笔记，地图，家）。当你遇到园艺卡住时，回到地图。当你陷入架构时，你必须回到杂草丛中。
> 
> And the **ARC** framework unifies everything!ARC 框架统一了一切！
> 
> Not only does it show Idea Emergence, it let's anyone see where they are getting stuck with the flow of their ideas, and immediately how to get unstuck.它不仅展示了想法的涌现，还让任何人看到他们的想法流在哪里卡住了，以及如何立即摆脱困境。
